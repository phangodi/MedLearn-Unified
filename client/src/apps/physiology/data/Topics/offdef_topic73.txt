// Identify the steps in the biosynthesis, storage, and secretion of T3 and T4
officialDefinitions: [
  'Overview of hormone synthesis: T4/T3 are synthesized in the TG EXTRAcellularly, in the colloid!!! Steps: 1. Iodide trapping (uptake). 2. Thyreoglobulin (TG) synthesis and secretion. 3. Organification of iodine and coupling. 4. Storage. 5. Endocytosis and proteolysis of TG, release of free T4/T3. 6. Deiodination of T4 to T3 or rT3 (in the thyroid and target cells). TSH-R activity stimulates all steps save the storage!',
  'Follicle: lined with single layer of epithelium, height depends on activity (cubic shape on average). Apical membrane microvilli — large surface area — site of synthesis.   'Synthesis and iodination of thyreoglobulin (TG): 660 KD (!!) Tyr rich (~100 aa) glycoprotein consisting two equal-sized subunits. Secreted by exocytosis into the colloid. Iodination of TG takes place extracellularly at the luminal membrane of follicular cells in the colloid.',
  '4 components of iodination reaction: 1. TG, 2. Iodide, 3. Thyreoperoxidase (TPO) a membrane bound enzyme (complex) and 4. dual-oxidase 2 (DUOX). Steps: 1.   'The coupling reaction: iodothyronine formation. Transfer of iodophenol moiety from one iodotyrosine to the phenolic OH group of a second iodotyrosine residue.   'TPO has pharmacological inhibitors: Thionamides (Propylthiouracil, Methimazole, Goitrin).',
  'Release of T4/T3: TG uptake through pinocytosis ("colloid droplets"). Enzymatic proteolysis in phagolysosomes — liberation of T4/T3, MIT, and DIT into the cytoplasm. T4 will be in part converted to T3 by deiodinase (5\' deiodinase, typ. I.) enzyme (TSH sensitive). Both T4 and T3 pass the basolateral membrane with facilitated diffusion (MCT8). Recycling of iodine: MIT/DIT deiodination by the iodotyrosine-dehalogenase. Iodide and tyrosine will be available for de novo TG synthesis (MIT, DIT can not be used).',

  'The products of the follicles: T4 and T3: special amino acids. 3,5,3\'5\'-Tetraiodothyronine (thyroxine, or T4) — Prohormone. // **LO-4** (Topic-73)
// Describe the plasma transport proteins involved in blood transport of thyroid hormones.
// Explain the importance of thyroid hormone binding in blood on free and total thyroid hormone levels
officialDefinitions: [
  'Transport in blood: T4>>T3 10:1 ratio in blood. 99.98% of circulating T4 and 99.5% T3 are protein-bound, 0.02 and 0.5% FREE in plasma (10-25 and 2-7 pmol/L).',
  'LIVER is responsible for the production of thyroid binding globulin (TBG), transthyretin (TTS) and albumin.',
  'Changes in TBG affect blood total hormone levels, but not free hormone levels. Large protein-bound fraction ensures even delivery.',
  'Activation of hormones in peripheral tissues: Thyroid hormones: Thyroxine → Triiodothyronine. Transport of hormones in the circulation: Lipid-soluble hormones: free + protein-bound form. Transport proteins: prealbumin, albumin, globulins. Only free hormones are biologically active, but bound and free fractions are in equilibrium.',
  'Thyroid hormones (T3 & T4) and steroid hormones circulate in the blood primarily bound to plasma proteins (e.g., albumin, thyroxine-binding globulin (TBG), corticosteroid-binding globulin (CBG)). Bound hormones are biologically inactive because they cannot cross the capillary walls to reach their target cells. Only the free, unbound fraction of the hormone is available to interact with target cell receptors and exert physiological effects.',
  'Protein binding prolongs hormone half-life and slows down hormone degradation and elimination, ensuring long-lasting effects. Thyroid hormones (T3 & T4) have a longer half-life (1-6 days) due to extensive binding with plasma proteins.',
  'Since bound hormones serve as a reservoir, they help maintain stable hormone levels and prevent fluctuations. When free hormone levels drop, more hormone dissociates from its binding protein to restore equilibrium, reducing the need for immediate hormone synthesis.',
],

  'T3/T4 effects: calorigenic effect. Increased thermogenesis, core temperature, basal metabolic rate, oxygen consumption. Plays a role in climatic cold adaptation. Mechanism: Increased ATPase activity (Na-K ATPase, other metabolic ATPases), increased thermogenin (UCP) expression (brown adipose tissue, skeletal muscle mitochondria), increased futile metabolic circuits (e.g., glycogenesis-glycogenolysis).',
  'T3/T4 effects: carbohydrate metabolism. Increased intestinal glucose absorption (postprandial hyperglycemia). Increased hepatic glycogenesis, gluconeogenesis, hepatic and muscular glycogenolysis. Increased glucose oxidation. Increased metabolism of glucoseaminoglycans (GAG)-s (myxedema).',

Increased cholesterol biosynthesis (HMG-CoA reductase) and elimination (LDL receptors). Net effect: decreased serum cholesterol. Increased bilirubin and beta-carotin metabolism.',
  'Thyroid hormones (T3, T4): Increase BMR. Hyperthyroidism raises BMR, while hypothyroidism lowers it.',
